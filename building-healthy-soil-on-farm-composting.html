Building Healthy Soil: The Importance of On-Farm Composting

By MooreHill Farm and Garden, LLC

At MooreHill Farm and Garden, healthy crops begin with healthy soil. One of the most important tools we use to improve soil health is compost.

Composting is the natural process of recycling organic materials—such as crop residues, leaves, grass clippings, vegetable scraps, and animal manures—into a nutrient-rich soil amendment.

Our composting process begins with collecting farm waste including overripe produce, crop residues, and other organic materials that would otherwise be discarded.

Using an on-farm composting system allows us to recycle nutrients, reduce waste, improve soil structure, and build long-term soil health.

Compost: An Investment in the Future

At MooreHill Farm and Garden, we believe that caring for the soil is one of the most important things a farmer can do. Healthy soil grows healthy food, and healthy food helps build healthy communities. Every season that we add compost to our fields, we are investing in future harvests and a more sustainable farming system.
