Building Healthy Soil: The Importance of On Farm Composting

By MooreHill Farm and Garden, LLC

At MooreHill Farm and Garden, healthy crops start with healthy soil. Compost is one of the most useful tools we have for taking care of the ground that takes care of the crop.

Composting is the natural process of turning organic material into something the soil can use again. Crop residue, leaves, grass clippings, vegetable scraps, and animal manure can all become a rich soil amendment when they are managed the right way.

Our process starts with collecting material from around the farm, including overripe produce, crop residue, and other organic matter that would otherwise be thrown away.

Keeping that process on the farm helps us recycle nutrients, reduce waste, improve soil structure, and build healthier beds over time.

Collecting overripe produce and crop residue for compost.

Managing organic material as it begins to break down.

Watching moisture closely so the compost can work the way it should.

Adding finished compost back into the growing beds.

Building organic matter in permanent beds.

Preparing healthier beds for future planting.

Bringing in bulk compost resources when the farm needs more organic matter.

Spreading compost as a family effort.

Distributing compost carefully so each bed gets steady fertility.

Treating compost as one of the best investments we can make in the farm.

Compost: An Investment in the Future

At MooreHill Farm and Garden, caring for the soil is part of the work every season. Healthy soil grows better food, and better food helps support healthier families and stronger local communities. Every time compost goes back into the field, we are investing in the next harvest.
